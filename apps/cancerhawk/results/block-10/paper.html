Despite these advances, lineage‑specific variability remains a formidable barrier to clinical translation. Addressing this lineage‑specific variability requires a multi‑pronged strategy. These failure modes are not mutually exclusive. If curvature variability is unaccounted for in the model while CPI observability is already compromised, the combined effect could amplify mistiming errors, leading to a complete breakdown of the scheduling strategy. Such failures would necessitate a reevaluation of the convex geometric framework, potentially requiring lineage-specific curvature models or more sophisticated multi-reporter systems to disentangle commitment signals from noise.
