1. Introduction

Cancer therapeutics development has entered a regime of diminishing returns that is, we argue, an epistemological problem more than a chemical one. The dominant readouts of the field — bulk and single-cell RNA sequencing, endpoint viability assays, Cell Painting and other single-frame morphological profiles, spatial transcriptomics fixed at a moment of sacrifice — all collapse time. Yet the decisions that determine whether a tumor cell lives, dies, invades, or evades are dynamical: they occur over seconds to days and they commit or abort at points of no return that leave no unambiguous signature in a snapshot. A cell at the restriction point and a cell that has just slipped mitosis can be transcriptionally near-identical; a BAX-translocation event that will kill a cell in forty minutes is invisible to a viability readout taken at seventy-two hours; a persister that will reseed a tumor in six months is, at the moment of drug withdrawal, morphologically unremarkable. The literature of the last decade has accumulated a menagerie of such commitment phenomena — mitotic slippage versus catastrophe under spindle assembly checkpoint pressure, DTP (drug-tolerant persister) emergence with its characteristic GPX4-dependent lipidomic rewiring, T cell–tumor immunological synapse productivity as a function of dwell time and calcium oscillation geometry, nuclear envelope rupture and repair during confined migration, ferroptotic wave propagation across epithelial sheets, and the collective-to-amoeboid plasticity of invasive fronts — and each of these is a trajectory, not a state. Static omics can describe the terrain; it cannot describe the path.

We propose that the appropriate computational object for cancer biology is therefore a learned simulator of cellular dynamics, and that recent video diffusion and flow-matching architectures — trained on multi-channel live imaging spanning subnuclear fluorescent reporters through organoid brightfield through intravital two-photon — now make such simulators tractable. We call this program **cell cinema**. The central conceptual move is to stop using deep networks as classifiers of what a cell *is* and start using them as generative priors over what a cell population *will do* under specified conditions. A conditional video diffusion model, given an initial frame stack, a perturbation token (a compound structure, a CRISPR guide, a cytokine milieu, a mechanical boundary condition), and a scale context, produces a distribution over plausible future trajectories. This is a counterfactual engine in the Pearl sense: it answers "what would this cell have done had I inhibited CDK4/6 at G1/S instead of at M?" rather than "what pathway is enriched in these reads?" The distinction matters because therapeutic decisions are interventional, and interventional questions require models that can roll forward under untested interventions while remaining faithful to the physical and biological constraints seen during training.

Three capabilities follow from a working cell-cinema prior and organize the rest of this paper. First, **in silico perturbation screening**: trajectories can be generated for combinatorial perturbations that exceed wet-lab throughput by orders of magnitude, with the model's predictive uncertainty itself guiding which combinations merit experimental confirmation. Second, **mechanism nomination**: because the model learns a latent dynamics, we can interrogate which latent coordinates control commitment transitions — the anaphase-onset manifold, the persister-entry basin, the synapse-productivity attractor — and map these coordinates back to measurable molecular correlates, producing falsifiable mechanistic hypotheses rather than gene-set enrichments. Third, **patient stratification on dynamical phenotypes**: a biopsy-derived organoid filmed for forty-eight hours under a small panel of perturbations yields a trajectory fingerprint that, projected into the generative model's latent space, predicts response to a much larger space of untested regimens.

The rest of this paper develops this program across scales. We organize twenty concrete research directions from subnuclear events (chromatin domain dynamics, nuclear envelope mechanics, mitotic fidelity) through single-cell commitment (apoptosis, ferroptosis, senescence entry, persister conversion) through multicellular behavior (collective invasion, immune engagement, stromal remodeling, clonal competition in organoids) up to intravital tumor dynamics. For each, we specify the imaging substrate, the conditioning scheme, the counterfactual query of therapeutic interest, and — critically — the failure mode that would falsify the approach. The unifying claim is neither that video generation will replace mechanistic biology nor that scale separation can be ignored; it is that a common architectural prior, trained across scales and modalities, offers the first computational framework in which the temporal commitments that actually govern tumor fate can be queried, perturbed, and, eventually, redirected.

2. Background

Three independent technical maturations have converged in a way that makes a generative theory of cell dynamics tractable rather than aspirational, and the case for pursuing it now rests on their simultaneity.

The first is the arrival of video generative architectures at a capability threshold where conditional spatiotemporal synthesis is no longer a novelty. Stable Video Diffusion, Lumiere's space-time U-Net, CogVideoX, and the broader family of video diffusion transformers (video DiTs) have demonstrated that a learned prior over pixel dynamics — when conditioned on text, image, action, or latent embeddings — can extrapolate physically plausible futures over hundreds of frames, preserve object identity across occlusion, and interpolate between distant states. Crucially for biology, these architectures have absorbed the inductive biases that make temporal extrapolation well-posed: factorized spatial/temporal attention, flow-matching and rectified-flow objectives that respect continuity, and classifier-free guidance that admits multi-modal conditioning. The same machinery that learns to hallucinate a pouring liquid from a caption can, in principle, learn to hallucinate a mitotic slippage from a drug embedding and a chromatin state. Nothing in the architecture privileges macroscopic scenes; the prior is agnostic to what "object" and "motion" mean, which is precisely why it can be retargeted to cells, organelles, and tumor spheroids.

The second maturation is the industrialization of live-cell imaging. Incucyte platforms generate continuous brightfield and fluorescence trajectories across thousands of wells; the Opera Phenix and comparable spinning-disk confocal systems deliver multichannel, multi-z, multi-well time-lapses at throughputs compatible with chemical and genetic libraries; and lattice light-sheet microscopy resolves subcellular dynamics — mitotic spindle mechanics, nuclear envelope rupture, mitochondrial fission — at isotropic resolution with photon budgets that permit hours of observation. A single modern campaign can yield on the order of 10^4 multichannel trajectories, each 10^2–10^3 frames deep. This is the first moment in which the data volume matches the sample complexity demands of video-scale generative modeling; Cell Painting-era still-image datasets, though transformative, were off by orders of magnitude in temporal content.

The third is the parallel maturation of matched perturbation libraries that supply the conditioning signal any generative model requires. JUMP-CP standardizes compound and CRISPR perturbations across consortium labs with shared morphological readouts; LINCS provides transcriptional responses across cell lines and doses; genome-wide CRISPRi/a arrays deliver loss- and gain-of-function at scale; and barcoded clonal systems such as Watermelon and ClonMapper enable retrospective linkage of fate to lineage, so that a trajectory's terminus — persistence, proliferation, death — can be mapped back onto its initial conditions. The conditioning alphabet is therefore no longer a bottleneck: chemical structures, sgRNA identities, concentration, exposure time, and clonal barcode can all be embedded and injected as cross-attention keys.

What makes this convergence consequential is that the underlying biology has been telling us for two decades that trajectories, not endpoints, are the causal substrate of therapeutic response. The Gascoigne–Taylor competing-networks model of mitotic catastrophe (2008) showed that cell fate under antimitotic drugs is determined by a race between cyclin B degradation and apoptotic threshold — a kinetic contest invisible to any endpoint assay, which records only who crossed the finish line. The persister literature from Sharma, Shaffer, and Hangauer established that drug-tolerant states are transient, reversible, and transcriptionally non-stationary: a population rewires over days, and the identity of surviving cells cannot be inferred from their pre-treatment expression profile alone. Mackenzie, Harding, and Bakhoum's cGAS-STING-CIN axis revealed that the inflammatory and metastatic consequences of chromosomal instability depend on the kinetics of micronucleus formation and envelope rupture — when and how often, not merely whether. Aceto's work on circulating tumor cell clusters demonstrated that metastatic potency is a property of multicellular trajectories in shear flow, with cluster cohesion and transient disaggregation dictating seeding efficiency. Denais and Raab showed that nuclear envelope rupture during confined migration is a stochastic, repairable event whose frequency distribution — not its mean — governs genomic damage accumulation.

Each of these findings identifies a commit point, a point of no return, or a rate-limiting transition that exists only in the time domain. Endpoint phenomics, however well-resolved in channels and morphology, is structurally incapable of representing them; it is the biological equivalent of judging a symphony from its final chord. A generative prior over cell video, conditioned on perturbation, is the natural object that can: it represents the full conditional distribution of trajectories, admits counterfactual sampling, and exposes the commitment manifolds where therapeutic leverage actually lives. The remainder of this paper develops that object and the experiments that would falsify it.

3. Mechanism

The central mechanistic claim of this paper is that video diffusion models, trained on sufficient longitudinal imaging of cancer and immune cells, do not merely interpolate pixels but recover a *dynamical manifold* on which the committed states of oncology — transcriptional addiction, ferroptotic collapse, mitotic catastrophe, persister entry, cytotoxic licensing, invasive escape — are geometrically localized. The manifold hypothesis here is stronger than in static generative biology: because video priors must obey temporal causality and conservation, their latent trajectories approximate the reaction coordinates of the underlying cell-state dynamics, and the free energy minima they encode are the same attractors a medicinal chemist is trying to reach. We operationalize this scale by scale.

At the subnuclear scale, MED1/BRD4 transcriptional condensates exhibit liquid-like coarsening whose exponent (classical Lifshitz–Slyozov α≈1/3 versus arrested glass-like regimes) is itself a biomarker of transcriptional-addiction fragility: super-enhancer–driven oncogenes such as MYC, MYB, and RUNX1 depend on a narrow window of condensate stability, and a generative prior conditioned on BET-inhibitor or CDK7i exposure should predict the critical coarsening slowdown that precedes collapse. The same prior, applied to lamin-perturbed or micronucleated nuclei, should learn NE rupture-repair dwell-time distributions; the right tail of that distribution — ruptures persisting long enough to expose chromatin to cytoplasmic cGAS — sets the cell-autonomous IFN threshold that licenses STING-dependent immunogenicity. A falsifiable prediction follows: in silico lengthening of predicted rupture dwell times, via counterfactual conditioning on ESCRT-III loss, should generate videos whose downstream ISG expression (decoded by a linear probe on the latent) matches measured IFN-β induction within a calibrated error.

At the organelle scale, mitochondrial networks undergo stereotyped fission–fusion choreographies whose topology — DRP1-driven peripheral fission versus midzone fission, OPA1-mediated cristae remodeling, contact-site dynamics with ER tubules — encodes, we hypothesize, a latent ferroptotic commitment coordinate. The video prior should learn that the transition from reticular to fragmented-with-swelling morphology, combined with lipid-peroxide-reporter flicker statistics, is not merely correlated with but predictive of the GPX4-inhibitor point of no return, distinguishable from apoptotic fission by its characteristic loss of fusion attempts.

At the single-cell scale, the manifold must discriminate mitotic fates that current endpoint assays conflate. Chromosome congression jitter, spindle pole oscillation amplitude, and cortical actin wave period during a prolonged metaphase arrest should, in the learned latent, separate catastrophe-bound from slippage-bound trajectories before nuclear envelope reformation commits the outcome. More consequentially, we predict the existence of *bottleneck frames* in the post–targeted-therapy response: short temporal windows (plausibly 6–24 h post-EGFRi or BRAFi) during which the latent trajectory funnels through a low-dimensional passage before diverging toward death, drug-tolerant persistence, or reversible dormancy. Identifying these bottleneck frames is the generative analog of finding the transition state in a chemical reaction, and the timing of a second-hit perturbation — MEKi, AXL blockade, GPX4i — relative to that frame should matter more than its identity.

At the two-cell scale, the immunological synapse is a spatiotemporally rich object whose video-scale features — LFA-1 ring closure kinetics, MTOC translocation velocity, Ca²⁺ oscillation frequency spectrum, lytic granule convergence and polarization — we posit form a sufficient statistic for functional outputs that are currently measured only indirectly: neoantigen TCR affinity, CAR-T serial-killing capacity, and the commitment to an exhausted transcriptional program. A prior trained on synapse cinema should, given a peptide-MHC sequence embedding as conditioning, generate synapse videos whose decoded kinetics predict killing efficiency; conversely, synapses that visibly fail to close the LFA-1 ring within a learned threshold should forecast NR4A/TOX-driven exhaustion hours before it is transcriptionally detectable.

At the multicellular scale, leader-cell emergence in collective invasion, clonal collision dynamics in mixed-barcode spheroids, and the gait signatures of circulating tumor cell clusters (rolling versus tumbling versus amoeboid streaming) constitute dynamical signatures of metastatic competence and organ tropism that the prior should bind to transcriptional states through paired live-fixed datasets.

The unifying therapeutic claim — and the one on which the rest of the paper depends — is that because these manifolds are learned by a differentiable generative model, they admit *operations*: geodesic interpolation from a treated cell's current latent state to the nearest death attractor identifies the minimal dynamical perturbation required; arithmetic over perturbation embeddings (druguantity_A + drug_B − drug_A ≈ drug_B context transfer) predicts synthetic-lethal combinations without enumerative screening; and conditioning on intervention timing allows in silico scheduling experiments whose falsifiable output is a wet-lab dose-timing curve. Failure mode: if the learned manifold is dominated by imaging nuisance variables rather than mechanism, latent arithmetic will fail to transfer across cell lines and microscopes, and bottleneck frames will not be reproducible — an outcome we explicitly test for in Section 6.

4. Proposed Experiment

We organize the empirical program as a tiered falsification lattice in which each tier probes a distinct scale of the dynamical manifold posited in Section 3 and, critically, each tier specifies an ex ante prediction, a causal perturbation that could refute it, and a numerical threshold below which the generative prior is declared to have failed at that scale. The architecture is shared across tiers: a video diffusion backbone (video DiT, ~3B parameters) is pretrained on a pooled corpus of live-cell microscopy and fine-tuned per tier with a conditioning head that accepts chemical structure (Morgan + learned), genetic perturbation (sgRNA target embedding), and, where relevant, paired omics.

The first tier is **mitotic cinema**. H2B-mCherry/α-tubulin-GFP HeLa, RPE1, and two patient-derived GBM lines (one near-diploid, one CIN-high) are imaged at 2-minute resolution across a panel of ~200 antimitotics spanning MPS1, KIF18A, PLK1/4, Eg5, CENP-E, and Aurora A/B inhibitors at ≥5 concentrations. The model is asked to predict, from the first 30 minutes of prometaphase dynamics, whether a cell will undergo catastrophe, slippage, normal division, or micronucleation, and to predict CIN-selective synergies (MPS1i+KIF18Ai; MPS1i+PLK4i) by latent-space composition of single-agent trajectories. Falsification: AUROC <0.80 for four-way fate prediction at t=30 min, or Spearman ρ <0.5 between predicted and measured Bliss excess across the ~2,000 combinations assayed at a 7×7 matrix subset. The causal test is a lagging-chromosome optogenetic rescue via LacO/LacI-CENP-E recruitment in predicted-catastrophe cells: the model must predict which cells are rescuable.

The second tier, **persister cinema**, uses lineage-barcoded PC9 (EGFR L858R) and A375 (BRAF V600E) populations under osimertinib and vemurafenib, imaged continuously for 14 days with matched retrospective scRNA-seq on persister and parental clones recovered at defined frames. The prior must predict which barcoded lineages enter a persister state from the first 24 hours of post-drug morphodynamics, with AUROC ≥0.75 benchmarked against static-frame CNN baselines; it must further predict, from persister-state video embeddings, the transcriptional program (AXL-high vs. NRF2-high vs. diapause-like) at r ≥0.6 against ground-truth scRNA-seq. The causal test is a prospective combination: the model nominates one co-treatment per lineage class, and we measure differential eradication by barcode abundance.

The third tier, **organoid synthetic-lethality cinema**, applies CRISPRi libraries (~500 genes enriched for KRAS effectors, MYC co-dependencies, and replication-stress nodes) combined with a ~50-compound panel in KRAS-mutant PDAC and CRC organoids, imaged by confocal at 1-hour cadence over 96 hours. Latent-space arithmetic — subtracting the monotherapy trajectory vector from the combination vector — must predict Bliss and HSA synergy at ρ ≥0.55, exceeding a gradient-boosted baseline on mean intensity features by ≥0.15, with prospective validation on 20 held-out combinations including a triplet nomination.

The fourth tier, **synapse cinema**, images TIL and CAR-T conjugates with LifeAct-GFP, LAMP1-RFP, and GCaMP6f against matched tumor targets, conditioned on TCR sequence and pMHC structural embeddings (e.g., AlphaFold-Multimer). The model must predict productive lytic licensing from the first 60 seconds of synapse formation at AUROC ≥0.78 and, prospectively, predict which synapses can be rescued or aborted by optogenetic Ca²⁺ clamping (CatCh or Opto-STIM1); falsification is failure of the predicted clamp-sensitivity map to correlate (ρ <0.4) with measured lysis outcomes.

The fifth tier, **collective-motion cinema**, uses 4D light-sheet imaging of MMTV-PyMT mammary organoids and barcoded multi-clone CRC mixtures to interrogate whether the prior captures emergent invasion dynamics not reducible to single-cell behavior. The central counterfactual is clone-ablation: the model predicts the invasive trajectory of the mixture after in silico removal of one barcoded subclone, and we validate by DTR-mediated diphtheria toxin depletion of that subclone in vitro. Agreement threshold: structural similarity of invasion front geometry (Hausdorff distance below a pre-specified bound) in ≥70% of ablation experiments.

The sixth tier, **organelle and subnuclear cinema**, probes whether the manifold extends below the cellular scale. Nuclear envelope rupture–repair is tracked with Lamin-A-GFP, cGAS-mCherry, and an IFN-β transcriptional reporter in CIN-high lines; the prior must predict which rupture events commit to cGAS engagement and IFN-β induction from rupture-moment geometry alone (AUROC ≥0.75). Mitochondrial choreography with MitoTracker and BODIPY-C11 must predict ferroptotic commitment ≥30 minutes before lipid peroxidation saturation. Endogenously tagged MED1, BRD4, and RPB1 condensate dynamics must predict transcriptional burst outcomes at a target locus (MS2-tagged MYC) with ρ ≥0.5 against nascent-RNA ground truth, with a prospective 1,6-hexanediol dissolution experiment as causal probe.

Across all six tiers, the unifying pre-registration specifies that failure at any two tiers constitutes refutation of the cross-scale manifold claim; success at ≥4 tiers, including at least one causal perturbation test per successful tier, constitutes provisional corroboration and defines the substrate on which Section 5's therapeutic-design loop is built.

5. Predicted Results

We expect the falsification lattice of Section 4 to yield a sharply tiered outcome pattern, and the structure of those tiers is itself informative: it delineates where temporal priors carry irreducible signal from where they merely re-express what static profiles already encode.

At the mitotic tier, we predict that trajectory-conditioned predictions of catastrophe versus slippage will reach AUC > 0.8 on held-out perturbations in the regime where the apoptotic threshold is not the gating variable — that is, in cells with intact BAX/BAK and a competent SAC, where commitment is set by the ratio of cyclin B1 degradation rate to cohesin cleavage timing. We expect the video prior to recover this ratio implicitly from chromosome oscillation amplitude and spindle-pole dwell-time in the 20–90 minute window before anaphase onset, features that are by construction inaccessible to endpoint assays. The prediction fails, and should fail, in MCL1^high or BCL-xL–amplified backgrounds where the death decision is downstream and stochastic relative to the mitotic trajectory; we expect AUC there to collapse toward 0.6, and this collapse is diagnostic rather than disappointing.

At the immune-synapse tier, we predict that kinetic features of the cytotoxic synapse — lytic-granule convergence velocity, MTOC polarization dwell-time, and the oscillation frequency of calcium flux at the interface — will predict productive killing with Spearman ρ in the 0.5–0.7 range against single-cell killing readouts, and will outperform NetMHCpan-based affinity rankings by ΔAUC ≥ 0.1 on a defined subset of intermediate-affinity neoantigens (predicted K_D 100–500 nM). The mechanistic reasoning is that for high-affinity epitopes, affinity saturates productive engagement and static prediction suffices; for low-affinity epitopes, nothing engages; the dynamic regime in between is where synapse persistence and serial-killing kinetics dominate outcome, and this is precisely the regime that peptide-MHC scoring systematically misreads. A corollary prediction: TCR affinity-matched pairs that differ in predicted synapse dwell-time by >2-fold should show divergent in vivo clearance in matched adoptive-transfer experiments.

At the nuclear-envelope tier, we predict that rupture-repair dwell-time — the interval between initial NE breach and ESCRT-III–mediated sealing, visible as BAF/LEM-domain recruitment kinetics — will correlate with bulk IFN-β induction at Pearson r > 0.6 under acute cGAS-activating perturbations (nuclear envelope rupture by confinement, lamin A/C depletion, or micronucleation after low-dose taxane). The dwell-time integrates cGAS access to genomic DNA, and because cGAS activation is switch-like above a DNA-length threshold, we expect the relationship to be sigmoidal rather than linear; video priors should recover the inflection point within ±15% across cell lines.

We anticipate moderate rather than transformative performance on latent-space arithmetic for synthetic-lethal pair discovery. Direction vectors derived from "BRCA1-loss trajectory minus BRCA1-wildtype trajectory" applied to candidate perturbation embeddings should yield 5–10× enrichment of known SL partners over random selection, but only 2–3× over static Cell Painting–based L1000 embeddings — because the synthetic-lethal signal is substantially encoded in steady-state stress morphology that static profiling already captures. The incremental value of the video prior here is narrow and specific: SL pairs whose phenotype manifests only transiently, such as replication-stress-dependent interactions that resolve within one cycle, should be preferentially recovered by the temporal model, and this subset is our falsifiable enrichment claim.

The highest-upside and weakest-prior predictions concern counterfactual intervention timing. We predict that video-prior–guided scheduling of dasatinib pulses during CAR-T manufacturing — timed to the predicted exhaustion-commitment frame rather than a fixed hour — will extend in vivo persistence by ≥50% over fixed-schedule controls in matched xenograft cohorts, and that epigenetic co-drug administration (e.g., EZH2 or KDM5 inhibition) delivered at the predicted persister-bottleneck frame in EGFR-mutant NSCLC models will reduce persister-pool size by ≥3-fold relative to continuous co-administration. These are the predictions most likely to fail outright, because they require the manifold to support actionable counterfactuals rather than merely descriptive ones; but their success would relocate therapeutic optimization from dose-response to dose-timing, a shift whose magnitude justifies the risk.

The unifying prediction across tiers is that video priors contribute unique variance specifically where the outcome variable is a temporal derivative — a rate, an oscillation frequency, a dwell-time distribution, a commitment latency — and contribute essentially nothing beyond static baselines where the outcome is a steady-state attractor. We expect this dichotomy to be observable within single tiers as well: within the mitotic tier, for instance, catastrophe-versus-slippage (a timing problem) should be well-predicted while final ploidy state (an attractor) should not exceed karyotype-based priors. The pattern, if it holds, constitutes the strongest evidence that what we are recovering is a dynamical manifold rather than a high-dimensional morphology atlas in motion.

6. Failure Modes

The falsification lattice of Section 4 will, we expect, fail in structured ways, and the structure of those failures is itself diagnostic. Six recurring modes organize our risk analysis, each tied to a specific scale of the dynamical manifold and each admitting a concrete mitigation that converts the failure from a dead end into an instrument.

The first and most fundamental is *molecular invisibility*. Pixels, however densely sampled in time, do not encode the biochemical state variables that often gate outcome: p53 tetramerization status, Mcl-1 versus Bcl-xL dependency, the mitochondrial BH3-priming index, TCF7 transcription factor occupancy in a stem-like CD8 T cell, or the chromatin identity of a micronucleus (cGAS-competent euchromatin versus silenced heterochromatin). A video model asked to predict whether an anaphase-bridged cell will mount a type-I interferon response will confabulate confidently on morphology alone, because the committed decision lives in a covalent landscape the camera cannot see. The mitigation is explicit molecular-state conditioning: auxiliary heads trained to predict paired single-cell RNA-seq, CITE-seq surface proteomes, and BH3-profiling readouts from the same wells, forcing the latent to carry the chemistry the pixels omit. Where the molecular head's uncertainty dominates, the video prior is flagged as under-determined rather than wrong.

*Shortcut learning* is the second mode and the one most likely to inflate held-out metrics while destroying combinatorial extrapolation. Video DiTs are exquisite pattern-matchers and will seize on photobleaching kinetics, channel registration geometry, cell-line–specific cytoplasmic texture, or plate-edge illumination gradients as proxies for condition labels. The failure announces itself when predictions collapse on unseen drug combinations or unseen cell-line backgrounds despite in-distribution performance. Biology-grounded discriminators — FUCCI-aware cell-cycle classifiers, lineage-tracer–consistent trajectory critics, and adversarial site/batch scramblers — force the representation to carry phase, identity, and commitment rather than nuisance covariates.

Third is *off-manifold hallucination*, the cell-biological analog of anatomically impossible hands. Latent interpolation between, say, an anaphase configuration and a ferroptotic bleb can produce a visually compelling intermediate that no real cell occupies — a chromosome geometry incompatible with kinetochore count, a plasma membrane lipid peroxidation pattern that coexists with intact mitochondrial cristae. Such hallucinations are dangerous precisely because downstream perturbation nomination treats them as achievable targets. A manifold-projection constraint, trained on a library of lineage-traced real trajectories with an energy-based score, rejects candidate futures whose tangent vectors fall outside the empirical flow field.

Fourth, and perhaps the most consequential for therapeutic design, is *correlation-not-causation*. The dynamical features the model locks onto — mitochondrial fission cadence preceding apoptosis, immune-synapse Ca²⁺ oscillation frequency preceding cytotoxic licensing, nuclear envelope fluctuation before rupture — may be faithful *reporters* of upstream commitment rather than *levers*. A model that predicts death from fission geometry will correctly rank cells and incorrectly nominate DRP1 as a therapeutic node. Optogenetic and chemogenetic causal tests are therefore not optional: CRY2-clustered DRP1, LOV-gated calcineurin, and photoactivatable BAX must be used to independently drive the candidate dynamical variable and verify that the predicted outcome follows. Interventions that pass prediction but fail causation are reclassified as biomarkers.

Fifth is *temporal aliasing*. cGAS engagement with cytosolic DNA can commit on sub-minute timescales; transcriptional bursts of resistance genes such as AXL or NGFR are rare, short, and decisive; the initial peptide-MHC dwell time at an immune synapse is sub-second. A 5-minute imaging interval, imposed by phototoxicity budgets, will silently smooth these events into absence. The Nyquist failure is invisible to standard validation because the ground truth is aliased identically. Adaptive-optics and event-triggered high-frame-rate acquisition — where a low-rate sentinel channel arms a burst-mode capture on detection of nuclear envelope deformation or synapse formation — restores the missing frequencies where they matter, at the cost of throughput.

Sixth, and most humbling, is *state-not-sequence dominance*. For a subset of oncology decisions the outcome is effectively determined before imaging begins: a TCF7⁺ stem-like CD8 cell will expand on checkpoint blockade and a terminally exhausted TOX^high cell will not, largely irrespective of the synaptic dynamics subsequently observed; a persister pre-committed by ancestral KDM5B^high state will survive whatever drug trajectory unfolds. Here the sequence-conditioned model is not wrong — it is under-specified, asked to predict from a variable with little remaining variance to explain. The diagnostic is a variance-decomposition analysis in which prior transcriptomic state is regressed out before scoring the video prior's residual predictive power; tiers where that residual is negligible are honestly reported as state-dominated, and the generative effort is redirected toward trajectories where dynamics genuinely arbitrate fate. Synapse-locked conditioning, in which the model is trained only on the window bracketing a defined commitment event, further concentrates capacity on the sequence-sensitive regime.

These six modes are not exhaustive but they are, in our experience with preliminary pilots, the ones that recur. Each mitigation converts a silent failure into a loud one, which is the minimum standard a falsification lattice must meet.

7. Translational Implications

The value of a video-generative prior to oncology is ultimately measured not in held-out likelihood but in whether it changes what moves from bench to bedside, and in which order. The dynamical manifold, if it exists as Section 3 posits and survives the falsification lattice of Section 4 at even a subset of tiers, enters the translational pipeline at six distinct points, each exploiting a different affordance of the learned prior and each producing a deliverable that is difficult or impossible to obtain from the static modalities it supplements.

The earliest point of entry is pre-experimental triage. Combination-therapy space is combinatorially intractable for wet-lab screening, and the existing priors used to prune it — synergy scores on viability endpoints, pathway overlap, expression correlation — are blind to the temporal ordering that determines whether two agents cooperate or merely coincide. A video prior that has internalized how mitotic trajectories narrow under KIF18A inhibition in CIN-high backgrounds can rerank MPS1i/KIF18Ai schedules by the predicted width of the lethal window before a single plate is seeded, and can flag antimitotic pairings whose therapeutic index widens only under specific interphase-duration distributions. The deliverable is not a drug but a rank order with calibrated uncertainty, consumed by screening committees that currently operate on intuition.

The second entry point is the dynamical biomarker, and it is the one most likely to generate near-term regulatory traction because it requires only that a short pre-treatment live-imaging window of a biopsy or leukapheresis product be run through the encoder. Condensate coarsening rates in BRD4/MED1 foci, extractable from minutes of high-NA imaging, should stratify BET and CDK7 trial populations more sharply than transcriptional addiction scores because coarsening integrates the biophysics that the transcriptional readout only implies. NE-rupture dwell-time distributions, similarly extractable, provide a pre-treatment estimate of cytosolic dsDNA flux and thus of STING-agonist responsiveness in combination with PD-1 blockade — a pairing currently selected by tumor mutational burden, a proxy known to fail in micro-satellite-stable disease. Mitochondrial choreography, specifically the fission-fusion duty cycle and cristae remodeling cadence, offers a ferroptosis-susceptibility readout that GPX4 expression alone cannot provide because it captures the flux, not the level, of the lipid-peroxidation buffering machinery.

The third entry point is manufacturing-stage QC for adoptive cell therapy, where the economic and clinical stakes of release decisions are acute. A CAR-T product whose synapse videos, scored by the prior, show truncated serial-killing cadence or premature exhaustion-like calcium decay predicts in vivo failure before infusion, converting a currently post hoc correlate into a release criterion. The same encoder, applied to patient material pre-manufacturing, identifies T-cell starting populations whose dynamical competence predicts expansion success.

The fourth entry point is temporally rational dosing. If synapse #2 is the point at which CAR-T cytotoxic programs commit to serial-killing versus exhaustion, a dasatinib pulse timed to that frame — rather than delivered as a continuous suppressor — may rescue exhaustion-prone products without blunting the first kill. If persister entry has a bottleneck frame in the chromatin-accessibility trajectory, an EZH2 or LSD1 co-drug delivered at that frame collapses the bottleneck, whereas the same drug delivered earlier or later is inert or antagonistic. These are scheduling hypotheses that correlation-based pharmacology cannot generate because they require a causal trajectory model.

The fifth entry point reframes combination design itself. Conventional combinations attack two nodes to reduce tumor-cell demography. Anti-cooperativity combinations, which the prior can identify by sampling counterfactuals over clonal-ecology trajectories, target the inter-cellular coupling — paracrine IL-6 handoffs, exosomal miRNA exchange, mechanical jamming — whose disruption collapses the ecology even when each clone remains individually viable. This is a categorically different therapeutic rationale and will require endpoint redefinition in trial design.

The sixth entry point is organ-tropism forecasting. CTC-cluster gait — the rotational, tumbling, or marginating dynamics under physiological shear — encodes arrest preferences that static cluster composition does not fully specify. A prior that predicts hepatic versus osseous versus pulmonary arrest from minutes of microfluidic imaging of a liquid biopsy informs surveillance imaging allocation and adjuvant regional therapy.

Cutting across these six is a design paradigm that merits explicit naming. Correlation-based repurposing asks which existing perturbation most resembles a desired transcriptional state. Video-generative counterfactual design instead specifies the *trajectory* — a serial-killing cadence, a collapse-mode rather than apoptotic death, a mitotic catastrophe without slippage — and inverts the prior to recover the perturbation, single or combinatorial, that produces it. The hypothesis is that trajectory-specified inversion will recover perturbations that state-specified matching cannot, because many clinically distinct outcomes share terminal states but differ in path; the failure mode is that inversion recovers only perturbations already in the training distribution, in which case the paradigm collapses to a more expensive form of repurposing and must be rescued by the active-learning loop of Section 4.

8. Conclusion

The argument of this paper reduces to a single wager: that the commitment events which decide oncological outcome — a mitotic cell choosing between catastrophe and slippage, a persister cell ratcheting into or out of a drug-tolerant attractor, a CD8 T cell licensing the cytotoxic synapse or disengaging, a clone winning or losing a mechanical confrontation at an invasive front — are not points in a static feature space but curves in a dynamical one, and that video generative models trained on enough live imaging recover that space as a usable manifold. Every preceding section is a consequence of taking that wager seriously. The mechanism section locates committed fates as basins on a learned trajectory manifold; the experimental lattice asks whether those basins are geometrically real by demanding that trajectory-conditioned generation predict perturbation responses the model never saw; the failure-mode analysis treats the molecular-invisibility gap, the causal-confounding trap, and the distributional brittleness of video priors not as caveats but as instruments that, when they fire, quantitatively map the residual information that pixels cannot carry. The translational section is then not a leap but a bookkeeping exercise: once a counterfactual simulator of cell futures exists at even one scale, each of its affordances — trajectory biomarkers, digital-twin dose scheduling, combination design by basin-hopping, synapse-resolved CAR engineering, persister-exit perturbations — follows from the same manifold geometry, applied with different conditioning inputs.

What distinguishes this program from the prevailing correlation-mining idiom of computational oncology is that its claims are physics-shaped. A learned prior over cell dynamics succeeds or fails by extrapolation under intervention, not by goodness-of-fit to held-out observations drawn from the training distribution. That framing imports both the discipline and the leverage of the physical sciences: a correctly tuned prior can, in principle, simulate regimes no assay has ever visited — a triple combination at a dosing schedule never administered, a synapse geometry never engineered, a persister population never chemically exited — with uncertainty that is itself calibrated against the manifold's local curvature. No static profiling modality, however deep its throughput, offers that affordance, because the operations required (time integration, causal intervention, counterfactual rollout) are not defined on snapshots.

The program's practical attractiveness lies in how its cost structure interacts with its falsifiability. Each of the twenty sub-hypotheses is individually executable at roughly $150K–$500K over six to eighteen months, uses reagents and imaging platforms already deployed in well-equipped cancer centers, and terminates in a numerical threshold that either advances the manifold hypothesis at that scale or retires it. Negative outcomes are not wasted: a tier that fails at its stated AUC nonetheless produces the first benchmarked live-imaging video corpus of its kind, quantifies the morphology-to-molecular information gap at that scale, and exposes which latent variables a future hybrid (imaging plus live reporters, plus perturbational transcriptomics) must carry. The asymmetry is favorable — bounded downside, a qualitatively new instrument on the upside — and it compounds across tiers because the backbone, the data pipeline, and the evaluation protocol are shared.

The timing argument is narrower than it may appear. Video diffusion reached conditional-generation fidelity adequate for scientific use only in the last eighteen months; high-throughput live-cell imaging with environmental control and perturbational multiplexing became routine at similar timescales; genome-scale and combinatorial perturbation libraries with matched imaging readouts are a post-2022 phenomenon. None of these alone would suffice; their simultaneity is what makes the manifold hypothesis newly testable. Windows of this kind — where three orthogonal technical maturations align before any single community has fully exploited the intersection — are historically when the largest interdisciplinary returns are available, and they close as the constituent fields routinize the intersection and reframe its questions in their own native vocabularies.

If the wager is right at even a subset of tiers, the deliverable is not another profiling layer bolted onto the existing stack but a different kind of object: a counterfactual simulator of cell futures, addressable by perturbation, queryable by desired endpoint, and falsifiable against the next live experiment. That object would occupy a position in oncology analogous to what molecular dynamics occupies in structural biology or what climate models occupy in geophysics — an imperfect but intervenable surrogate for a system too expensive to query exhaustively, whose value lies precisely in the regimes where direct measurement is impossible. Cell cinema, in this view, is not a visualization layer over cancer biology. It is the first attempt to put the field's decisive variables — time, commitment, counterfactual — into an instrument that can be operated.
