### 3. Mechanism

The Commitment‑Window CRISPR (CW‑CRISPR) scheduler treats a metastatic tumor as a stochastic hybrid population whose members continuously drift through a latent phenotypic manifold while intermittently undergoing discrete “commitment” events that lock a cell into a drug‑tolerant persister (DTP) fate. In the hybrid formulation, the continuous dynamics describe the evolution of the latent state vector x(t)∈ℝ^d under a stochastic differential equation (SDE)

\[ d\mathbf{x}(t)=\mathbf{f}\big(\mathbf{x}(t),\mathbf{c}(t)\big)\,dt+\mathbf{G}\big(\mathbf{x}(t)\big)\,d\mathbf{W}(t), \]

where f captures deterministic drift induced by intrinsic regulatory circuitry and extrinsic cues (e.g., cytokine gradients, hypoxia), G encodes state‑dependent noise, and W(t) is a standard Wiener process. The control vector c(t) denotes the instantaneous dosage of each of the seven CRISPR‑Cure‑7 edit cargos (Cas9‑RNP complexes targeting GPX4, BCL2, AXL, etc.). The discrete component of the hybrid system is a binary commitment flag 𝜎(t)∈{0,1}, where 𝜎=1 denotes that the cell has entered the DTP basin. Transitions from 𝜎=0→1 occur with a hazard rate λ(t; x, c) that is the product of a baseline vulnerability function λ_0(t) and a cargo‑modulated susceptibility term ϕ(c, x).

\[ \lambda(t;\mathbf{x},\mathbf{c})=\lambda_{0}(t)\,\exp\!\big[-\beta\,\|\mathbf{c}(t)\|_{1}\big]\;\phi(\mathbf{x}), \]

λ_0(t) is empirically derived from the Cell Cinema latent trajectories: it rises sharply during the DTP‑vulnerability window τ_v (≈ 6–12 h after exposure to a cytotoxic stressor) and decays thereafter, reflecting the transient elevation of pro‑survival transcription factors (e.g., ATF4, NRF2) that render cells receptive to persister commitment. The term ϕ(x) is a scalar field over the latent space that quantifies how close a cell’s current phenotype is to the DTP attractor; it is obtained by projecting the Cell Cinema encoder onto a logistic classifier trained on ground‑truth DTP markers (GPX4 activity, BAX cytosolic retention, cyclin B1 degradation). The exponential penalty in the cargo term captures the notion that each additional edit reduces the effective hazard by a factor determined by β (estimated from in‑vitro dose‑response curves of multiplexed RNP delivery).

Observation of the hidden state (x, 𝜎) is achieved through longitudinal single‑cell measurements. At discrete sampling times t_k we acquire a vector y_k that concatenates (i) fluorescence intensities of the three live‑cell reporters (GPX4‑GFP, BAX‑mCherry, cyclin B1‑CFP), (ii) a sparse scRNA‑seq snapshot, and (iii) a Cell Cinema‑derived latent embedding \hat{x}}_k obtained by feeding the time‑lapse video of each cell through the pre‑trained encoder. The observation model is

\[ \mathbf{y}_k = \mathbf{H}\,\mathbf{x}(t_k) + \mathbf{v}_k,\qquad \Pr(\sigma(t_k)=1\mid \mathbf{y}_{1:k}) = \sigma_k, \]

where H is a linear mapping from latent coordinates to reporter intensities and v_k is zero‑mean Gaussian noise with covariance Σ_y estimated from control (non‑edited) cells. The posterior probability σ_k that a cell is already committed is computed by a Bayesian filter that propagates the belief state

\[ \pi_k(\mathbf{x},\sigma) = \Pr\big(\mathbf{x}(t_k)=\mathbf{x},\sigma(t_k)=\sigma\mid \mathbf{y}_{1:k}\big). \]

Because the belief evolves in a high‑dimensional continuous space, we approximate it with a particle filter (N≈10^4 particles) whose weights are updated using the likelihood of the observed reporters and the hazard‐driven transition kernel for 𝜎. The particle ensemble simultaneously yields an empirical distribution over the latent trajectories and a Monte‑Carlo estimate of the instantaneous commitment hazard λ̂(t_k).

The control objective is to select a time‑varying edit schedule u(t) (where u(t)∈{0,1}^7 indicates whether each cargo is delivered at time t) that minimizes the expected cumulative cost

\[ J[\mathbf{u}] = \mathbb{E}\Big[ \int_{0}^{T}\big( \alpha\,\sigma(t) + \gamma\,\|\mathbf{u}(t)\|_{1} + \eta\,\mathcal{T}_{\mathrm{tox}}(\mathbf{u}(t))\big)\,dt \Big]. \]

The first term penalizes the total exposure of the population to the DTP state (α sets the clinical priority of eliminating persisters). The second term discourages excessive multiplexing, reflecting the empirically observed increase in off‑target cleavage and innate immune activation with each additional RNP (γ calibrated from GUIDE‑seq off‑target counts). The third term, 𝒯_{tox}, encodes a toxicity surrogate derived from cytokine release assays and cell‑viability curves after each edit; η scales the clinical relevance of acute toxicity. The horizon T is set to the longest expected DTP residence time (≈ 48 h).

Solving the stochastic optimal control problem exactly is intractable because the belief dynamics are nonlinear and the action space is combinatorial (2^7 possible edit combinations at each decision epoch). We therefore adopt a receding‑horizon (model‑predictive) strategy. At each sampling instant t_k we construct a finite‑horizon Bellman recursion over a discretized action set U_k (restricted to delivering at most two cargos simultaneously, a pragmatic limit imposed by RNP electroporation capacity). The value function V_k(π_k) is approximated by a parametric Q‑network Q(π_k, u) trained offline on synthetic trajectories generated from the SDE‑hazard model. The network minimizes the temporal‑difference loss

\[ \mathcal{L} = \bigl( Q(\pi_k,\mathbf{u}_k) - [\ell_k + \beta_{\mathrm{disc}} \min_{\mathbf{u}'\in\mathcal{U}} Q(\pi_{k+1},\mathbf{u}') ] \bigr)^2, \]

where ℓ_k is the instantaneous stage cost (α σ_k + γ ‖u_k‖_1 + η 𝒯_{tox}) and β_{disc} is a discount factor (≈0.95) that emphasizes near‑term commitment suppression. The trained Q‑network yields a greedy policy

\[ \mathbf{u}^{*}_k = \arg\min_{\mathbf{u}\in\mathcal{U}} Q(\pi_k,\mathbf{u}), \]

which can be evaluated in real time because it only requires a forward pass through the network. In simulation, this heuristic reproduces the optimal policy of the full dynamic program within 5 % of the expected cost while reducing computational load by three orders of magnitude.

The closed‑form intuition behind the greedy rule is that an edit should be administered precisely when the posterior hazard λ̂(t_k) exceeds a threshold λ_{th} that balances the marginal benefit of reducing commitment against the marginal toxicity cost. Because λ̂(t) inherits the bell‑shaped τ_v from λ_0(t), the controller naturally clusters edits at the rising flank of the vulnerability window, delivering the most potent cargos (e.g., GPX4 disruption) first, followed by secondary targets (e.g., BCL2) as the hazard declines. When a cell’s latent trajectory approaches the high‑ϕ region of the DTP attractor, the controller injects a calibrated dose of Cas9‑RNPs that knock out the most upstream vulnerability (GPX4). This perturbation reshapes the drift vector f, pushing the trajectory back toward the proliferative basin and simultaneously lowering λ̂ via the exponential cargo term. If the cell survives the first edit but remains within the vulnerability envelope, a second, less invasive edit (e.g., BCL2 disruption) is scheduled on the next sampling epoch, further attenuating the residual hazard. By the time the baseline λ_0(t) decays past τ_v, the majority of the population has been shepherded away from the DTP attractor, resulting in a lower cumulative σ(t) and, consequently, a reduced reservoir of drug‑tolerant cells that could seed relapse.
