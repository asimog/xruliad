## Abstract

**Abstract**  
Metastatic relapse is driven by disseminated tumor cells (DTCs) that occupy drug‑tolerant epigenetic attractors, whose persistence is encoded in the kinetic barriers of the underlying chromatin landscape. By casting stochastic epigenetic dynamics in the Otto‑metric formalism, we model the ensemble of chromatin accessibility, transcription‑factor occupancy, and histone‑modification states as a time‑dependent Wasserstein manifold Γₜ. Ollivier–Ricci curvature computed on single‑cell ATAC‑seq graphs provides a scalar field, Ric_f(∇τ,∇τ), that quantifies local resistance to state transitions: strongly negative curvature demarcates metastable basins that trap DTCs, whereas shallow curvature marks permissive corridors toward drug‑sensitive configurations. We derive a curvature‑adaptive control law that modulates CRISPR‑dCas9–TET1 editing flux inversely with the square‑root of the Fisher‑weighted Ricci field, thereby compressing epigenetic commitment windows. In silico simulations of a four‑dimensional Otto–Wasserstein landscape predict a 31 % reduction in mean first‑passage time from H3K27me3‑rich tolerant basins to H3K27ac‑enriched sensitive attractors, accompanied by a characteristic parabolic log‑hazard signature 2–3 days before phenotypic switching. Ex vivo validation in patient‑derived triple‑negative breast cancer organoids demonstrates a ≥40 % local curvature increase along targeted trajectories and a 1.8‑fold acceleration of chemosensitivity reversal relative to uniform editing. These findings establish Ricci curvature as a manipulable geometric observable that governs metastatic latency, offering a mechanistic blueprint for precision epigenetic therapies that preemptively dismantle drug‑tolerant reservoirs.
