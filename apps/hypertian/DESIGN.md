# Design System: Hypertian
**Project ID:** hypertian-web

## 1. Visual Theme & Atmosphere
Hypertian should feel like a creator-owned control room rather than a memecoin landing page. The atmosphere is dark, glassy, and technical, but not sterile. It leans on sea-glass cyan, broadcast blue, and restrained light bloom so the product reads as trustworthy infrastructure for people running live shows under pressure.

The interface should avoid generic AI startup tropes: no purple gradients, no empty fake analytics, no oversized neon glows, and no soft consumer-app whimsy. Visual energy comes from layered depth, disciplined spacing, and a few strong contrast moments around live-state information.

## 2. Color Palette & Roles
- Tidal Black (`#061015`) is the foundation for app backgrounds, overlay framing, and the deepest surfaces.
- Control Room Slate (`#091216`) anchors dense panels, sticky navigation, and elevated shells.
- Sea Glass Signal (`#7CE4D2`) is the primary action color for CTA buttons, active states, and success-adjacent emphasis.
- Broadcast Blue (`#86B6FF`) is the secondary accent for route labels, supporting highlights, and chart-adjacent emphasis.
- Soft Copy Mist (`#9DB6BA`) is the main secondary text color for explanatory body copy.
- Quiet Steel (`#6D8589`) is reserved for tertiary labels, helper text, and low-priority metadata.
- Frost White (`#F5FBFB`) is used sparingly for type that must feel immediate and high confidence.

## 3. Typography Rules
Space Grotesk is the primary interface face. It should be used for headings, navigation, buttons, and body copy so the product feels modern, editorial, and slightly assertive. IBM Plex Mono is the utility face for URLs, identifiers, wallet snippets, payment references, and other operational strings.

Headlines should run tight with negative tracking and high contrast. Supporting copy should breathe with relaxed leading and never feel cramped. Monospace text should remain compact and functional, used only where traceability matters.

## 4. Component Stylings
* **Buttons:** Primary actions are pill-shaped with a sea-glass to blue gradient and dark ink text. Secondary actions are translucent pills with a crisp border and quiet hover lift.
* **Cards/Containers:** Containers use generously rounded corners, layered translucent fills, and whisper-soft inner highlights. They should feel engineered, not frosted-luxury.
* **Inputs/Forms:** Inputs are dense and dark with clear borders, understated inset depth, and strong focus rings. They should feel reliable in live-operational contexts.
* **Status Chips:** Live, standby, and verification states use compact uppercase pills with subtle borders rather than loud badges.

## 5. Layout Principles
The layout should prioritize clarity under pressure. Major sections need room to breathe, but dashboards should stay compact enough that a creator can scan status quickly. Hero sections may be more expressive, while operational screens should remain disciplined and modular.

Use asymmetry deliberately: larger messaging blocks paired with tighter system panels. Avoid perfectly centered marketing layouts when the product is fundamentally about tools, control, and state awareness.

## 6. Motion & Background
Background motion should feel atmospheric and slow, like signal drift in a studio environment. Hover motion should be short and purposeful. Respect reduced motion settings everywhere.

## 7. Guardrails
- Do not use purple as a hero color.
- Do not revert to system fonts.
- Do not introduce fake benchmark numbers or invented customer logos.
- Do not overload screens with glows, shadows, or decorative chart motifs.
- Do keep the product feeling like serious creator infrastructure with crypto-native context.
